Pasyal Palawan

Palawan Cuisine

Tamilok

Tamilok looks a little strange at first because it comes from mangrove trees, but people in Palawan love it. It’s soft and slippery, kind of like an oyster, and usually dipped in vinegar with onions. Locals say it tastes better than it looks!

Crocodile Sisig

This is sisig made from farm-raised crocodile meat. The meat is firmer than pork but still tender when cooked right. It’s seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili, so it tastes familiar but still something new to try.

Danggit Lamayo

Unlike regular danggit, that is super dry, lamayo is only half-dried, so when you fry it, it becomes juicy and crisp at the same time. It’s a popular breakfast food in Palawan, usually eaten with garlic rice and vinegar.

Nido Soup

This dish uses edible bird’s nests gathered from caves in El Nido. The soup is clear, light, and a bit luxurious because it’s rare and delicate. It’s often served during special occasions.

Hopia Palawan

This hopia is known for being extra flaky and buttery. It has classic fillings like ube and mung bean, and travelers love bringing it home as pasalubong.

Halo-Halo sa Balinsasayaw

This version of halo-halo is famous because the serving is huge and the toppings are premium—like leche flan, ube, macapuno, and ice cream. It’s a perfect treat after touring around Palawan.

Fish Kinilaw

Fresh fish straight from the ocean is marinated in vinegar, calamansi, onions, and ginger. It’s sour, spicy, and super refreshing—almost like seafood ceviche but with Filipino flavors.

Grilled Tuna Panga

This giant tuna jaw is grilled until smoky and tender. It’s juicy, flavorful, and often shared because of its size. You dip it in calamansi-soy sauce for the full experience.

Chao Long

This came from the Vietnamese community that settled in Puerto Princesa. It’s a comforting noodle soup with soft rice noodles and tender beef or pork. The broth is slightly sweet, rich, and perfect when you add fresh herbs.

Lato (Sea Grapes) Salad

Lato is a type of seaweed that looks like tiny green beads. When you bite into it, it pops a little and tastes fresh and salty. People mix it with tomatoes and onions to make a simple, refreshing salad.
